Module Descriptions

Domain Driven Design

Goal:

To develop software that closely aligns with complex business domains, focusing on creating understandable, maintainable, and scalable solutions centered on domain logic.

Why it matters:

  • Master complexity in large projects.
  • Improve communication with a shared language.
  • Increase maintainability with modular systems.
  • Bridge the gap between technical solutions and business goals.

Data Structures & Algorithms

Goal:

To understand and apply the fundamentals of data structures and algorithms, emphasizing the selection of efficient solutions for performance and scalability.

Why it matters:

  • Significantly improve application performance.
  • Strengthen analytical and problem-solving skills.
  • Design robust, high-quality software components.
  • Justify technical choices with scientific evidence.

Software Design

Goal:

To learn new design techniques and reflect on existing ones, working from the smallest unit (a line of code) up to the entire system architecture.

Why it matters:

  • Gain a deeper understanding of development choices.
  • Increase maintainability by consistently using design patterns.
  • Create systems that are easier to understand and extend.

UX Engineering

Goal:

To build digital applications with a core focus on accessibility, expanding on knowledge from previous web development courses.

Why it matters:

  • Accessibility is a fundamental aspect of quality software.
  • It is a mandatory legal requirement for companies under the EU Digital Services Act.

The Software Engineering Project

A lecturer-proposed, real-world topic executed by a team of three. You will investigate unfamiliar technologies, make and defend engineering decisions, and deliver a working, documented, and tested solution.

Your Bachelor’s thesis (BAP) runs in parallel to capture the rationale, evidence, and reflection behind your project work.

Core Requirements

Code & Usability

  • Hosted on GitHub in a team-owned repo.
  • Deliver a ready-to-use artifact (app, library, etc.).
  • One-command setup (e.g., `docker compose up`).
  • Provide `env.example` and sensible defaults.

Documentation

  • Clear `README.md` with quick start & usage.
  • Installation and deployment guide.
  • Architecture overview.
  • `RESEARCH.md` to document your process.

Professional Workflow

  • Develop on feature branches.
  • Keep `main` branch protected and green.
  • CI pipeline on push/PR (lint, test, build).
  • Track work with GitHub Issues & Projects.

Research & Rationale

  • Follow the structured Research Flow.
  • Define objectives, scope, and success metrics.
  • Analyze existing solutions and alternatives.
  • Justify technology and architecture choices.

Project Workflow & Coaching

Project Coaching

Frequency
Weekly for the first two weeks, then bi-weekly.
Purpose
Progress review, scope alignment, and risk management.
Who Attends
The full team (20min format).
How to Prepare
Bring a short agenda, demo of latest work, and top blockers.
Outcomes
Agreed decisions and action items recorded in your repo.

Technical Coaching

Frequency
One half-day per week; book a slot.
Purpose
Deep technical unblocking and design guidance.
Who Attends
Relevant team members closest to the problem.
How to Prepare
Bring a minimal reproducible example, logs, and specific questions.
Etiquette
For targeted questions only; not a replacement for self-study.

The Bachelor's Thesis (BAP)

Running in parallel with your project, the BAP is your chance to dive deep into the "why" behind your work.

Purpose of the BAP

  • Document your reasoning and technical choices.
  • Argue your decisions with evidence and research.
  • Reflect critically on your engineering practice and project outcomes.
